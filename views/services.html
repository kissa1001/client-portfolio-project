Proofreading

Superficial mistakes distract your reader from great ideas; good proofreading helps your message shine through in the most clear and professional way!

What Does a Proofreading Edit Examine?
  • Sentence subject agreement
  • Capitalization
  • List form agreement
  • Spelling
  • Missing words
  • Typos
  • Correct word choice for meaning (diction)
  • Comma splices/sentence fragments/run-on sentences
  • Active phrasing and clarity
  • Punctuation, including proper hyphenation, quotation form, and apostrophe use

Content Edit

What Does a Content Edit Examine?

A comprehensive examination of idea-effectivenes in a piece of writing, including:

  • Best order of information: most important to least important, broad to detailed
  • Natural beginnings and end-points of paragraphs
  • Transitions between subtopics
  • Missing information/gaps in narrative
  • Motivation/ Cause-and-effect relationship clarity
  • Main ideas coming through for the reader
  • Any overarching content confusion

I work for Sherman Writing Services to provide content feedback on works of fiction and creative nonfiction:
“We provide constructive criticism to help writers recognize the thematic and structural strengths and weaknesses in their manuscripts. By offering new insight into how to improve the manuscript, we enable writers to focus their attention on necessary revisions and continue forward with preparing novels for submission or self-publishing.”-SWS
